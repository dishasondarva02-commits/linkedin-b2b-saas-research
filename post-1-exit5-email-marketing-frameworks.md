## LinkedIn Content Philosophy (observed from session)

### 1. Newsletter as LinkedIn Pre-Content
Dave explicitly references writing about topics on LinkedIn *before* discussing them in live sessions ("I actually just wrote about this earlier today on LinkedIn"). His LinkedIn and newsletter are tightly coupled — one feeds the other.

### 2. "Nobody Wants Your Marketing" Mindset
References Steven Presfield's *Nobody Wants to Read Your Sh\*t* as a core operating principle:
> "I assume that nobody wants my marketing and then therefore I'm going to take this pill — how do I work with that as the key factor."

This mindset drives his approach: every email, post, and session must justify its claim on the reader's time.

### 3. Counter-Intuitive Newsletter Insight He Promotes
> "Oh my god how dare a brand feature other people not affiliated with their brand — that's how you build trust."

Promotes linking to and elevating others' content in newsletters, which is counterintuitive for marketers trained to keep readers on their own properties.

### 4. Reply Rate > Open Rate
Dave publicly tracks *replies* to his newsletter, not open/click rates. After a newsletter on "over-complicating marketing," he received 100 replies — which he uses as a signal to create more content on that topic.

## Email Marketing Frameworks Curated from Exit5 Live Session

### Framework A — Personalized Year-in-Review (Ben, Quo)
- **Type**: Surprise-and-delight campaign to existing customers
- **Mechanic**: 110 lines of SQL → customer data buckets → personalized GIFs + real data graphs → shareable landing pages
- **Result**: High engagement, social sharing, brand credibility for SMB customers
- **Key insight**: Product data is underused marketing ammo. Manufacture your own news cycle from what users are already doing in the product.
- **B2B SaaS application**: Monthly analytics emails showing each customer their own usage data = recurring engagement without a campaign

### Framework B — B2B Newsletter Engine (Jana, Knack)
- **Type**: Consistent weekly newsletter with rotating bylines
- **Operating model**:
  - One quarterback (calendar, theme coordination)
  - Rotating writers from across the marketing team
  - Fixed template, variable content
  - Written 2–3 weeks ahead
- **Metrics that matter**: Reply rate and DMs, not open/CTR
- **Key insight**: Showing up in the inbox same day/time every week is a touchpoint even when unopened — treat it like a TV show with a fixed schedule
- **B2B SaaS application**: Feature other voices/communities' content in your newsletter — builds trust faster than self-promotion

### Framework C — Newsjacking Email System (Tyler, Hypermedia Marketing)
- **Type**: Automated relevance-driven email triggered by industry news
- **Stack**: Google Alerts → AI (Claude) scanning inbox → brief generation → copywriter review → client approval → send
- **Timeline**: Under 1 hour from news event to sent email
- **Result example**: 67.3% open rate, 4%+ reply rate for a pharmacy client responding to FDA regulatory update
- **Key insight**: Relevance + Speed = Attention. Long-sales-cycle B2B needs to stay top-of-mind between buying events.
- **B2B SaaS application**: Configure AI alert system for industry regulatory changes, funding news, competitor announcements — each is an email trigger

### Framework D — Newsletter Nurture Engine (Kmy, Fractional B2B)
- **Type**: Dual-track email system (nurture newsletter + conversion campaign)
- **Results**:
  - Small brand: 40–50% open rates, 2–3% CTR, ~$500K revenue influenced over 2 years
  - Enterprise brand: 20% reduction in unsubscribes, 40% increase in CTR in under 3 months
- **Key principle**: Give away value freely before expecting conversion; measure email engagement of buyers 10 weeks *before* they converted, not just at point of conversion
- **B2B SaaS application**: Track how many emails a contact opened in the 10 weeks before they became a paying customer — this reveals your nurture cadence's actual influence on pipeline

## Psychological Triggers in Dave's Content Approach
- **Anti-hype positioning**: Opens sessions by calling out AI hype, building trust with "real marketing" audience
- **Radical empathy**: "I assume nobody wants my marketing" — earns credibility with the audience before asking for anything
- **Community-first**: Exit5 is built on the principle that marketers learn best from other marketers doing the work, not vendors selling tools
- **Reply loop**: Publishing → audience replies → Dave shares insights back → strengthens community bond

## B2B SaaS Applications (Cross-cutting)
- The "no-reply@" email address is still killing B2B email trust in 2025 — send from a real human
- Your product data = your best marketing asset; build a monthly user analytics email campaign
- For long-sales-cycle B2B (6–18 months): newsjacking is the best tool to stay relevant between buying moments
- Measure newsletter success by replies and buyer email-engagement history, not vanity open rate metrics
