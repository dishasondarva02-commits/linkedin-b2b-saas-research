# Post 3 — MRR / Revenue Milestone (Numbers + Proof — Top Performer)
**Author:** Tyler Denk (Co-founder & CEO, beehiiv)
**Source:** Reconstructed from coaching session with Lara Acosta (YouTube)
**Content Type:** Milestone post — authority + social proof

## Why It's a Top Performer
Milestone/data posts are Tyler's most reliable top-performers. Key elements:
- **Specific numbers**: MRR, user count, growth percentages — concrete proof
- **Screenshot or graph**: Visual evidence makes the number credible
- **Not just bragging**: Always tied to a lesson or implication for the reader

## Reconstructed Post Structure

**Hook:** beehiiv just hit $[X]M in ARR. Here's what surprised me most about getting here.

**Body:**
- Where we started: [early number / context]
- What we expected: [the assumption]
- What actually happened: [the surprise / counterintuitive finding]
- What it taught us: [3 bullet points — the transferable lessons]

**Image:** Screenshot of MRR dashboard or growth chart (real data, not placeholder)

**CTA:** "If you're building in the creator economy — what's the one thing you wish you knew earlier?"

## Psychological Triggers Used
- **Social proof**: Hard numbers from a real company validate the authority to speak on the topic
- **Curiosity via surprise**: "Here's what surprised me" makes a milestone post a learning post, not just a flex
- **Aspiration + relatability**: The early number grounds it ("we started at $0 too") while the current number inspires
- **Image = proof**: Real screenshots signal authenticity in a feed full of AI-generated graphics

## B2B SaaS Applications
- Post milestone updates (MRR, users, NPS, retention) monthly — not just at round numbers
- Always include a "here's what we didn't expect" angle to convert a brag into a lesson
- Screenshots of actual dashboards (even with some numbers blurred for privacy) outperform designed graphics for credibility
- Milestone posts drive recruiting and partnership inquiries — track inbound via LinkedIn DMs, not just website traffic
