# Post Type — Product-First / Organic Growth Philosophy
**Author:** Grant Lee (Co-founder & CEO, Gamma)
**Source:** Reconstructed from YouTube interview with Lara Acosta
**Content Type:** Milestone announcement + thought leadership

## Core Content Pattern: "Tiny Team, Massive Output"

Grant engineered a repeatable sound bite — "tiny team that accomplishes a lot despite having very little resources" — and seeded it repeatedly across LinkedIn and founder content until the New York Times picked it up organically.

This is deliberate narrative engineering: repeat a sticky positioning phrase until external media amplifies it.

## Example Post Template (from interview reconstruction)

**Hook:** We just hit [milestone] with a team of [X] people.

**Body:**
- The common wisdom was [wrong assumption]
- What we actually did was [contrarian decision]
- The result: [proof/number]

**Lesson:** [One-line takeaway that others can apply]

**CTA:** [Soft question or tag to community]

## Growth Philosophy Posts (observed from interview)
- "We spent zero on advertising the first two years — here's what we did instead"
- "We relaunched our entire product in 4 months — what forced us to do it"
- "Before you spend on marketing, make the first 30 seconds magical"
- "We saw sign-ups plateau. Instead of throwing money at ads, we rebuilt the product."

## Psychological Triggers Used
- **Anti-advertising contrarianism**: "We spent $0 on ads for 2 years" as hook → validates the bootstrapped/lean-team audience
- **Process reveal**: Showing the decision-making behind milestones (the fork in the road) is more valuable than just announcing the milestone
- **Sound bites as narrative loops**: Deliberately planting quotable phrases that media and other creators amplify

## B2B SaaS Applications
- Every product launch decision can be a post: "why we rebuilt X instead of adding feature Y"
- The "first 30 seconds magical" philosophy applies to onboarding emails, landing pages, and product tours — each is LinkedIn content
- Track which phrases get repeated by others in comments/shares — those become your brand sound bites
