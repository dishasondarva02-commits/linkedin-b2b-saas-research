## Recurring Mechanisms Across All Sources

### 1. Specificity beats reach
Every single expert prioritizes a narrow, specific audience over maximum reach. LinkedIn's algorithm now explicitly matches content to interest profiles — broad content gets distributed broadly but shallowly; specific content reaches the right people deeply.

### 2. Transformation > Achievement
Lara Acosta (6-step storytelling framework), Grant Lee (build-in-public journey), and Kmy (newsletter personal note) all independently arrive at the same principle: the reader wants to see the transformation, not just the result. Announcing "we hit $10M ARR" is less powerful than "here's what happened when we hit $10M that we didn't expect."

### 3. Product data = underused marketing asset
Ben (Quo year-in-review) and Dave Gerhardt (explicit synthesis comment) both highlight that most B2B SaaS companies ignore the richest content source they have: their own product usage data. Personalized, real-data content outperforms all other email types.

### 4. Reply rate > Open rate
Jana (Knack), Kmy, and Dave Gerhardt all independently moved away from open/CTR as primary metrics. Replies and DMs are harder to measure but directly map to trust — the actual goal of nurture content.

### 5. Consistency as a compounding asset
Lara Acosta (repetition builds reputation), Jana (TV show cadence), and Dave Gerhardt all converge on: showing up consistently is more valuable than any single great piece of content. The algorithm "files" you based on consistent signals; humans build trust through repeated exposure.

### 6. Audience as distribution, not just readers
Lara Acosta (algorithm paper), Grant Lee (sound bite engineering), and Tyler (3+1 comment strategy) all frame the existing audience as a distribution mechanism, not just a readership. Shareability, comment-worthiness, and engagement habit are more strategically important than follower count.

## B2B SaaS-Specific Applications

### For early-stage (pre-product-market-fit)
- Focus on Type 2 posts (personality/story) — you don't yet have the numbers for Type 1
- Build the newsletter now; LinkedIn drives traffic, email retains it
- Borrow authority: tell stories of others solving the problem you're building for

### For growth-stage ($1M–$10M ARR)
- Add Type 1 posts (milestone/data) at each meaningful number
- Implement newsjacking for any industry regulatory or competitive news
- Engineer one sound bite that positions your company narrative

### For scale-stage ($10M+ ARR)
- Build a full content ecosystem: founder posts + employee posts + creator partnerships
- Personalized product-data emails (year-in-review, monthly analytics) for customer retention
- LinkedIn = top-of-funnel for customers, recruits, AND partners — track all three
