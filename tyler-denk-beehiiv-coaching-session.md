# YouTube Transcript Summary — Tyler Denk (beehiiv CEO) × Lara Acosta: LinkedIn Coaching Session
**Guest:** Tyler Denk, Co-founder & CEO, beehiiv (~$30M ARR, 110 employees, 15 countries)
**Coach/Host:** Lara Acosta
**Format:** Live content audit + strategy session

---

## Context
Tyler has ~58K LinkedIn followers vs. ~40K on X, but wants to reach 100K on LinkedIn. Lara analyzed 200+ of his posts over 365 days and ran a live coaching session to systematize his winning patterns.

---

## Part 1: The 5-Level Creator Framework (Lara's Audit Model)

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Beginner — 0 to first 1,000 followers |
| 2 | Intermediate — to 10K, testing, no clear niche |
| 3 | Consistent — posting regularly, seeing traction |
| **4** | **Tyler's level — credibility + proof + positioning, but missing consistency** |
| 5 | Final boss — systemized, repeatable, dominant |

**Tyler's gap**: Great high-performers, but sporadic posting and no repeatable framework.

---

## Part 2: Audit Findings — 3 Winning Post Types

From 200+ posts, 20 consistent top-performers shared these patterns:

### Type 1: Milestone / Data Posts
- MRR numbers, user growth, ARR milestones
- Always paired with a real screenshot or graph
- Hook: "beehiiv just hit [X]. Here's what surprised me."
- Why it works: **numbers = authority signal**; screenshot = proof; surprise framing = learning, not bragging

### Type 2: Personality / Selfie Posts
- Burnout, remote work, productivity, personal struggles
- Always paired with a candid selfie
- Total Addressable Market content: anyone who works can relate
- Why it works: **broadens top-of-funnel** beyond email/newsletter niche; makes Tyler rootable

### Type 3: Contrarian Educational Posts
- "Email is dead" (and why it isn't)
- "Remote work is superior to in-office" (counterintuitive for a CEO)
- Why it works: combines **authority + curiosity gap + topic familiarity**

---

## Part 3: The Systemization Approach

### The Templatization Workflow
1. Identify best-performing post format
2. Feed it to an AI tool (Cleo) as a "swipe file"
3. Input new milestone/context/update
4. AI generates a new post in the same proven structure
5. Human proofread + add proprietary insights AI wouldn't know
6. Post

**Time saved**: From hours to under 2 minutes per post.

### Key Insight
> "Once you find the winning format, you rinse and repeat it until it stops working — then find another through testing."

---

## Part 4: The 3+1 Comment Strategy

The only reliable way to "hack" the algorithm legitimately:

### In your post:
Drop 3 follow-up comments immediately after posting:
1. BTS (behind-the-scenes) photo or observation
2. A tip related to the post topic
3. Another BTS image or supplementary education

### On the feed:
Leave meaningful comments on others' posts (not just "great post!") — adds your name to relevant conversations.

### Reply to every comment within 30 minutes:
Early comment velocity = algorithm signal. More replies early → faster distribution.

---

## Part 5: Algorithm Mechanics

### Link placement
- **In the post** (not comments): higher traffic-driving conversion rate; use once per week for lead magnet or signup CTA
- **In comments**: if traffic isn't the goal; builds awareness without demanding the click

### Image type by post type
| Post Type | Best Image |
|-----------|-----------|
| Storytelling / personal | Selfie / candid photo |
| Educational / data | Infographic or screenshot |
| Product update | Screenshot of actual data (MRR, growth chart) |
| Contrarian hook | Text-only can work if hook is strong enough |

### Posting cadence recommendation
- 4 key posts/week using proven templates
- 1 educational/contrarian (most time-intensive) + 3 story/personality posts
- Reduces friction for founders who aren't "natural" educators

## Part 6: The SLAY Framework (Applied to Tyler's Case)

**S** – Story: "I spent six days in London and spoke to 100 of our customers."  
**L** – Lesson: "They all had one thing to say: email changed their business."  
**A** – Actionable: "80% of them monetize faster. They build credibility. They quit the 9-to-5."  
**Y** – You: "This is the power of email. When are you building yours?"
