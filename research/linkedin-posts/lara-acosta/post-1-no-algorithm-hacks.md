# Post 1 — "You don't need algorithm hacks to grow on LinkedIn"
**Author:** Lara Acosta
**Date:** ~6 months ago (edited)
**Type:** Systemic breakdown / framework post

## Content
You don't need algorithm hacks to grow on LinkedIn.

I spent the last 90 days testing every growth hack I could find. Here's what actually works (based on analyzing content in different niches):

**1. Hyper-specific content (3x a week)**
- Solve one exact problem per post
- Use industry language your clients use
- Speak directly to one person
- Example: "Stop losing credibility with your body language."
- Adam Robinson has mastered this for B2B. Nausheen I. Chen is killing it for B2C.

**2. Proof-First Content**
- 80% tactical step-by-step frameworks and screenshots (builds trust)
- 20% stories on how you're doing it (engages more)
- Educate and storytell — always both.

**3. LinkedIn Video**
- Video reach might be down, but it's the fastest way to prove authority.
- LinkedIn Lives are a goldmine — a live with 200 people generated $10,000.
- Recommends limiting lives to ~2/month.
- Includes a video-scripting checklist as an attached image.

Closing: "It's not about more volume, it's about more insights. Dominating LinkedIn isn't just about virality — it's about building a brand that earns trust."

CTA: Repost for a private "storytelling" video (limited to 20 people) + 50% off her programs.

## Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS
- Niche specificity > broad reach
- 80/20 split between tactical proof content and personal narrative
- Video/Lives as authority/trust-building, not primary reach driver
