# Post 5 — "My 6-step storytelling framework is unbeatable"
**Author:** Lara Acosta
**Date:** ~1 year ago (edited)
**Type:** Storytelling / transformation framework post

## Content
"If you cringe at writing stories on LinkedIn, read this: Most stories focus on the achievement or big 'win'. But the best ones focus on the transformation instead."

The 6-step framework with examples:
1. Write the 1 thing you want to talk about → "Giving a keynote at an industry conference"
2. Write down the 3 challenges you faced → "Struggled with mental health, wanted to quit, failed"
3. Write down what the challenges taught you → "Had to learn how to turn failures into strengths"
4. Write down how you overcame them → "Found mentors and focused on self-improvement"
5. Link it back to why it matters to your audience → "It all looked easy from the outside, but the reality..."
6. Leave people with one lesson they can apply → "Saying no to everything so I could say yes to me"

Closing: "Engage them with emotion, sell them with the facts."

## Key Takeaways for B2B SaaS
- Focus on **transformation**, not the achievement itself — directly maps to founder "milestone" posts (Grant/Tyler interviews show this in practice)
- Concrete fill-in-the-blank structure makes this usable as a repeatable template for product launches, fundraising stories, hiring milestones, etc.
- "Engage with emotion, sell with facts" — pairs storytelling (emotion) with proof/data (facts), echoing the proof-first 80/20 model from Post 1
