## Full Post Text

I walked away from a $1.1M business to build an agency.

Not because it was failing.
Because I finally found the idea I couldn't stop thinking about.

For years I was "the buyer psychology person."
Good. Respected. But not *known* for one thing.

Then I watched what happened when Justin Welsh owned "solopreneur systems."
When Sam Parr owned "business ideas."
When Dickie Bush owned "digital writing."

They didn't just have expertise.
They had an ownable idea — one strategic territory that made them impossible to ignore.

That's when I realized:
The fastest path to authority isn't more content.
It's one idea, compressed until it's sticky, spreadable, and unmistakably yours.

So I pivoted.
Customer Camp. Be Unignorable. The UNIGNORABLE newsletter.

Same psychology background.
Completely different packaging.

If you're posting consistently but still feel invisible, the problem probably isn't your work ethic.
It's that nobody can file you under one idea yet.

Fix that first. Everything else gets easier.

P.S. I'm hosting a live session on finding and shaping your ownable idea. Justin Welsh, Sam Parr, and Dickie Bush are joining as bonus resources. Link in comments.
