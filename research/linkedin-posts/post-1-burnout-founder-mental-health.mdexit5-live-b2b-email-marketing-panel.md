## Panelist 1: Ben — Quo (Customer Lifecycle)
**Campaign:** Personalized Year-in-Review ("Spotify Wrapped" for business phone users)

### What they did
- Segmented customers into 4 personas: Dial Dynamo, Message Maestro, AI Innovator, Platform Innovator
- Generated personalized animated GIFs via agency (Multiplied Media, South Africa)
- Pulled real usage data: call minutes, Sona usage, message count, busiest call time
- Created shareable landing pages per customer
- Targeted: owners and admins only (the analytics-facing roles)

### Tech stack
- Snowflake (SQL queries) → Multiplied Media (GIF creation) → Census/Pipedrive → Customer.io → QuickChart API (real-time chart generation) → shareable URLs

### Results
- High social sharing (customers shared stats on X and LinkedIn, all tagged Quo)
- Strong clickthrough for a personal/sensitive data email
- Reinforced loyalty with long-tenure customers; acknowledged top-percentile performers by industry

### Key Insight
> "In the absence of news, great marketing teams can manufacture a reason to talk about their product — by using the product data itself."

## Panelist 2: Jana — Knack (Head of Brand & Content)
**Campaign:** Weekly B2B Newsletter with rotating bylines

### Operating model
- Same day, same time, every week (TV-show consistency)
- One quarterback → calendars writers, coordinates with campaigns
- Rotating bylines: demand gen lead, content writer, brand designer all contribute issues
- Fixed template; only the content changes
- Written 2–3 weeks ahead

### Content philosophy
- 80% useful curated content (including third-party, non-affiliated sources)
- 20% brand promotional (blog posts, events, product updates)
- Personal "why this matters" commentary on every linked piece — not just link dumps

### Metrics that replaced open/CTR
- Reply rate (manually tracked via Help Scout, all replies to hello@[company].com)
- DMs and community mentions
- Qualitative feedback themes

### Key Insight
> "Even on the weeks people don't open it, the brand name in their inbox is still a touchpoint — and touchpoints compound."

## Panelist 3: Tyler — Hypermedia Marketing (B2B Agency)
**Campaign:** AI-powered Newsjacking Email System

### The problem it solves
Long B2B sales cycles (12–18 months) = risk of being forgotten between buying events. Newsjacking keeps the brand top-of-mind with timely, relevant sends.

### The system (5 steps)
1. **Google Alerts / alert monitoring** configured with niche-specific keywords per client
2. **AI (Claude)** scans the alert inbox, reads full articles (not just headlines)
3. **AI identifies** primary topic angles (direct relevance to audience) + secondary angles (brand awareness / trust-building)
4. **AI builds email briefs**: hook, topic, concept, design direction — 5 unique angles per article
5. **Human review**: copywriter reviews for accuracy, legal compliance, tone → client approves → send

**Total time: under 1 hour from news event to sent email**

### Example: Compound Pharmacy + FDA Update
- News: FDA removed semaglutide from shortage list → compounders can't produce it
- Action: OFA sued FDA → FDA modified policy
- Email sent within 1 hour: explained the situation, confirmed Southoun Pharmacy's compliance, CTA to contact account manager with questions
- Results: 67.3% open rate, 4%+ reply rate, very low unsubscribes

### Key Insight
> "Relevance + Speed = Attention. The trend doesn't have to be viral — it has to be relevant to your specific audience."

## Panelist 4: Kmy — Fractional B2B Email Marketer
**Campaign:** Dual-track newsletter + conversion email system

### Two brands, opposite starting points
- **Small brand** (~10K contacts, dormant list, almost no sends): introduced weekly newsletter → consistent 40–50% open rates, 2–3% CTR, ~$500K revenue influenced over 2 years
- **Enterprise brand** (100K+ contacts, too many conversion emails): slowed down, introduced nurture newsletter, added second weekly conversion email → 20% reduction in unsubscribes, 40% increase in CTR in under 3 months

### Content structure (Content Marketing Institute model)
1. Section 1: Campaign highlights / big company news
2. Section 2: Personal note — written by a named person with photo and title (peer-to-peer voice)
3. Section 3: Curated content picks with commentary
4. Section 4: Events / campaigns CTA

### Attribution model that worked
Tracked email engagement of buyers in the **10 weeks before they converted** — not just at point of sale. Found a consistent pattern: buyers had been lurking and opening emails regularly before ever clicking a CTA.

### Key Insight
> "Lurkers exist. They open every email. They never engage. Then one day they buy. Measure the accumulation — not just the conversion event."

## Cross-Session Takeaways (Dave's synthesis)
1. Email is the last channel you truly own your audience on — no algorithm between you and the inbox
2. Product data is underused marketing ammo — build your own news cycle from inside your product
3. Newsletters should give away free value before expecting anything in return
4. Reply rate is a better trust metric than open rate or CTR
5. For long sales cycles: newsjacking is the tool for staying relevant between buying moments
6. "No-reply@" addresses are still killing B2B email trust in 2025 — always send from a real human
7. Consistency of cadence is a touchpoint even when the email isn't opened
