# YouTube Transcript Summary — Grant Lee (Gamma Co-founder) LinkedIn Playbook
**Guest:** Grant Lee, Co-founder/CEO of Gamma (70M+ users in 2 years)
**Interviewer:** Lara Acosta
**Topic:** Founder-led LinkedIn strategy that built a B2B SaaS distribution engine

## Summary of Key Points

### Product-first philosophy
- Before any marketing: build a product with strong organic word-of-mouth. Gamma relaunched its product in March 2023 after a public beta plateau, focusing all hands on making the "first 30 seconds magical."
- Community building (in-person meetups, e.g., flying to London to meet 200 users) feeds founder-led content.

### Why LinkedIn over X
- Grant tested both LinkedIn and X early on; LinkedIn content resonated more for his audience and built momentum faster — "find one channel working and double down."

### Three content pillars
1. **Education** — visual communication / storytelling / copywriting / design / brand consistency topics, broken into testable sub-pillars
2. **Founder story / journey** — "build in public," lessons learned, reframing personal struggles (e.g., the Morris Chang/TSMC story as a way to avoid "cringe" by telling others' stories first)
3. **Announcements & milestones** — company launches, revenue milestones, partnerships, customer wins; mapped on a content calendar

### Posting cadence & system
- Aiming for 5x/week; consistency = more data points to test what resonates
- Builds a backlog of ideas (notes app), scores past posts weekly: was the hook good? Was the angle too generic or too niche?

### Cringe Valley
- Every founder goes through a low-engagement "cringe valley." Overcome it by telling others' stories first, then sprinkling in personal reflection ("halo effect").

### Virality formula (Grant's view)
- Mindset test for every post: "Would someone share this with a friend or bookmark it?" Shares/bookmarks > likes/comments for virality.
- Hook must be a scroll-stopper; body must be packaged so the takeaway is clear enough to share.

### Distribution results for Gamma
- LinkedIn = ~25%+ of cross-platform signups; also drives recruiting (people apply after seeing announcements) and B2B partnership inbound via DMs.
- "Sound bites" (e.g., "we're a tiny team") can become self-fulfilling narratives picked up by press (NYT) and other creators.

### Advice for new B2B SaaS founders
1. Find a peer group of founders to post alongside (accountability + feedback)
2. Be your own consumer — study what performs, reverse-engineer by rewriting good posts word-for-word
3. Ask for specific, critical feedback (e.g., after podcast appearances, ask "what one thing stuck with you?")

### SLAY framework (mentioned in this interview too)
- Story → Lesson → Actionable advice → "You" (CTA), used for milestone/announcement posts
