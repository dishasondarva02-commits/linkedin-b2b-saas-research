## Key 2025 Findings

### 1. Relationship Depth > Follower Count
The algorithm now weights content from accounts you've recently interacted with higher than content from large accounts you don't engage with. Comment strategy is an algorithm requirement, not optional.

### 2. Two Content Types in Feed
- **Connected content:** From people you follow (traditional)
- **Unconnected content:** Surfaced because someone in your network engaged with it — even from accounts you've never followed. Your network IS your distribution system.

### 3. Profile as AI Prompt
LinkedIn converts your entire profile (headline, experience, skills) into text fed to an AI matching system. Vague profiles = invisible to distribution.

**Bad:** "Marketing professional | Growth enthusiast"
**Good:** "I help B2B SaaS founders build LinkedIn pipelines without paid ads"

### 4. Format Performance (General Trends)
- Text-only posts from domain experts perform well when genuinely useful
- Carousels/document posts drive saves (high-intent signal)
- Video requires 3-second visual hook + ~500-word accompanying copy
- External links in post body still reduce reach vs. links in comments

### 5. Relevancy Over Recency
Posts get "second wave" distribution weeks after publishing if newly relevant — similar to YouTube's recommendation resurfacing. Good posts don't expire after 24–48 hours.

### 6. Small Account Advantage
The performance gap between old and new algorithm systems is largest for accounts with the fewest interactions — beginners benefit most from the shift toward relevancy matching.

## Posting Recommendations

| Factor | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Frequency | 3–5x per week, consistent schedule |
| Timing | Less important than consistency and relevance |
| Hashtags | 3–5 relevant, not 30 generic |
| Links | First comment only |
| Comments | Reply within 30 minutes of posting |
| Profile | Specific ICP statement in headline |

---

## Psychological + Algorithm Overlap

| Algorithm signal | Psychological principle |
|---|---|
| Early engagement velocity | Social proof (others are engaging, so I should too) |
| Relationship-weighted distribution | Mere exposure (familiar accounts get priority) |
| Save/bookmark signals | Commitment (high-intent action > passive like) |
| Profile-keyword matching | Cognitive ease (relevant content feels "meant for me") |

## B2B SaaS Application

1. Fix profile headline before posting anything else
2. Pick one lane — going off-topic hurts ALL content distribution
3. Build engagement habits with 30 target accounts
4. Post consistently for 90 days — evaluate at day 90, not day 9
5. Optimize for saves and comments, not just impressions
