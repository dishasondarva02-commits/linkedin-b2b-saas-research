## Panel 1: Ben (Quo) — Spotify-Style Year-in-Review Email

### What they built
A fully personalized year-in-review campaign for Quo (formerly OpenPhone) customers:
- Dynamic GIF hero image with 4 user buckets (Dial Dynamo, Message Maestro, AI Innovator, Platform Innovator)
- Real data visualizations (calls, Sona usage, call minutes, messages sent, busiest call time)
- Shareable landing pages for LinkedIn/X distribution

### Backend complexity
- 110 lines of SQL to build personalized CSV
- Snowflake → agency (Multiplied Media) → Customer.io → QuickChart API for individual charts
- Verification steps before send

### Results & intent
- **Campaign type:** Surprise and delight (not direct upsell)
- **Target:** Owners and admins who see platform analytics
- **Social loop:** Users shared stats on LinkedIn/X, tagging Quo — cross-pollination without SEO push
- **Key insight from Dave:** "This is the 10x content version of email" — effort compounds into shares and brand awareness

### B2B SaaS application
Product data is marketing ammo. When you lack external news, manufacture a micro-news cycle from internal usage data. Personalized proof beats generic feature announcements.

**Psychological triggers:** Identity affirmation (you're a "Message Maestro"), social proof (top 2% in your industry), pride of ownership (shareable stats).

## Panel 2: Jana (Knack) — B2B Newsletter as Trust Channel

### Core thesis
> "If your B2B brand doesn't have a real newsletter — not a digest or campaign blast, but thoughtful, unique content — you're leaving one of the best trust channels on the table."

### Three reasons it worked (previous company, weekly newsletter)
1. **Cadence** — Same day, same time every week. Even unopened emails = brand touchpoint. "Marketing teaches us you need a stack of touch points to land a customer."
2. **Trust through curation** — Not just own content. Featured wider email marketing community voices. Elevated others = elevated brand.
3. **Reply rate as primary metric** — Stopped optimizing for open/click rates. Measured replies, DMs, and community mentions instead.

### Operating model (4 pillars)
1. Single owner ("quarterback") who calendars writers and ties themes to campaigns
2. Rotating bylines from team members (demand gen lead, content writer, brand designer)
3. Fixed template — only content changes
4. Work 2–3 weeks ahead

### Dave's amplification
- Replies earn future trust — responding to newsletter replies proves a human is behind it
- Counterintuitive trust move: linking to other people's content in your newsletter builds more trust than self-promotion
- "Nobody wants to read your [stuff]" mindset — empathy for the inbox drives quality

### B2B SaaS application
Pair LinkedIn zero-click posts with a weekly newsletter that delivers depth. LinkedIn builds familiarity (mere exposure); newsletter builds trust (consistent value). Reply rate > open rate as a signal.

**Psychological triggers:** Reciprocity (free value before asks), mere exposure (weekly cadence), social proof (community curation).

## Panel 3: Tyler (Hypermedia Marketing) — Ryan Reynolds Newsjacking System

### Framework: Newsjacking for long sales cycles
For B2B with 12–18 month sales cycles, the challenge is staying relevant between buying windows. Tyler's system collapses trend-to-send time to under 1 hour.

### 5-step system
1. **Alert monitoring** — Google Alerts (or SparkToro's Alertmouse) configured per client niche: job titles, industry topics, regulatory updates
2. **AI inbox scanning** — Claude scheduled task reads alert digest folder automatically
3. **Angle generation** — AI identifies primary topics (direct relevance) and secondary topics (brand awareness play)
4. **Email brief creation** — AI email writer skill builds hooks, concept, design brief
5. **Human review + send** — Copywriter QA, legal review if needed, client approval

### Case study: Southoun Pharmacy (503A compound pharmacy)
- FDA removed semaglutide/tirzepatide from shortage list
- OFA sued FDA; policy shifted
- Email sent within 1 hour: clear explanation, compliance reassurance, CTA to reply to account manager
- **Results:** 67.3% open rate, 4%+ reply rate, very low unsubscribes

### Dave's insight
Premium email design matters even for news updates — not a plain-text "hey did you know" but a polished, high-end product experience.

### B2B SaaS application
Speed + relevance = trust during long cycles. AI compresses research-to-send time. For SaaS, monitor regulatory changes, competitor moves, and industry news that affects your ICP — then be first to explain what it means for them.

**Psychological triggers:** Urgency (timely news), authority (first to explain), loss aversion (what the news means for their business).

## Panel 4: Kmy — Newsletter Restructuring at Two Scales

### Two brand contexts
1. **Small brand (~10K dormant list):** Went from occasional sales sequences to weekly nurture newsletter → 40–50% open rates, 2–3% CTR, ~$500K revenue influence over 2 years
2. **Enterprise brand (100K+ list):** Reduced email volume, diversified content types → 20% unsubscribe reduction, 40% CTR increase in 3 months

### Newsletter structure (Content Marketing Institute model)
1. Campaign highlights / research / big content pieces
2. **Personal note** — written as one person to one person, with photo, name, title
3. Events and conversion CTAs come *after* the personal note

### Core principle
> "You are willing to give away tons of free value before you expect anything in return."

Gatekeeping IP kills email performance. Earn the right to ask for engagement.

### Creative engagement measurement
Instead of only tracking CTA clicks, track how many emails buyers opened in the 10 weeks before purchase. "Lurkers" who never reply still accumulate trust through touchpoints.

### Dave's closing principles
- "You-focused" language: "You will learn X" not "We are hosting X"
- Assume nobody wants your marketing — then work backward from empathy
- Email is one of the last channels you truly own

### B2B SaaS application
Restructure email from conversion-blast to nurture-first. One weekly value email + one optional conversion email. Personal note from founder/CEO builds founder-led brand continuity from LinkedIn to inbox.

**Psychological triggers:** Reciprocity, cognitive ease (personal note format), commitment (consistent weekly rhythm).
