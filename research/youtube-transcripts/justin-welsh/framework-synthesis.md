## Core Frameworks

### 1. Pillar-Spoke Content System
- **Pillar:** One deep piece of content per week (newsletter essay, long-form thread)
- **Spokes:** 5–7 derivative LinkedIn posts extracted from the pillar
- **Efficiency:** One idea → one week of content

### 2. 2-Hour Content Batch
- Block 2 hours weekly to write all content
- Schedule everything in advance
- Eliminates daily "what should I post?" decision fatigue

### 3. Newsletter → LinkedIn Funnel
- Newsletter subscribers are the warmest audience
- LinkedIn posts drive newsletter signups via profile link
- Newsletter content feeds spoke posts

### 4. Solopreneur Identity Positioning
- Reframed "small" from limitation to intentional choice
- Sold identity (freedom, control) not features (content templates)
- Ownable idea: "the one-person business"

## Post Structure Patterns

| Element | Welsh's approach |
|---|---|
| Hook | Single punchy line — curiosity gap or contrarian statement |
| Body | Short paragraphs (1–2 lines each), numbered lists |
| Value | Actionable framework or system |
| CTA | Soft — "follow for more" or question prompt |
| Links | Never in post body |

---

## Psychological Triggers

- **Curiosity gap:** Opens with incomplete information that demands resolution
- **Identity aspiration:** "Intentionally small" identity frame
- **Cognitive ease:** Numbered lists, short paragraphs, scannable structure
- **Social proof:** Revenue numbers, follower counts used sparingly as proof points

---

## B2B SaaS Application

- Adapt pillar-spoke for product education: one deep case study → 5 LinkedIn posts
- Batch content weekly; don't create daily under pressure
- Frame product as enabling an identity ("the founder who hires like a pro")
- Profile CTA → newsletter → nurture → demo
