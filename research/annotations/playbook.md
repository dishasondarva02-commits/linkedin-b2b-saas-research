## Layer 1: Positioning First (April Dunford)

**Before writing a single post, answer:**
1. What would customers do if your product didn't exist? (competitive alternatives)
2. What do you do that alternatives can't? (unique attributes)
3. Who cares most about that difference? (target segment)
4. What category frame makes the value obvious? (market frame)

**Playbook rule:** If you can't explain your positioning in one sentence, no amount of LinkedIn volume will convert.

## Layer 2: Buyer Psychology (Katelyn Bourgoin)

**JTBD content formula:**
- Map 3 ICP types → job to be done → obstacle → hire moment
- Write trigger event posts for passive lookers (stages 1–2 of buying timeline)
- Run 5 clarity call interviews → extract exact buyer language for hooks

**Psychological trigger bank:**
| Trigger | When to use | Example hook angle |
|---|---|---|
| Loss aversion | Problem recognition | "You're losing candidates and don't know it" |
| Identity threat | Awareness | "You're not bad at hiring. Your tools are." |
| Mere exposure | Trust building | Consistent posting on same 3–5 subtopics |
| Rumpelstiltskin Effect | Problem naming | Coin and define a named syndrome |
| Social proof | Validation | Customer metrics, milestone posts |

## Layer 3: Content Architecture (Justin Welsh + Lara Acosta)

**Justin Welsh — Pillar-spoke system:**
- 1 pillar topic per week (long-form newsletter or deep post)
- 5–7 spoke posts derived from the pillar (LinkedIn-native)
- Batch content in 2-hour sessions

**Lara Acosta — SLAY + repetition system:**
- **SLAY:** Story → Lesson → Actionable advice → Yes/Question
- Pick 1 topic + 3–5 subtopics; rotate angles weekly
- Match format to content-founder fit (logical vs. storytelling)

**Weekly cadence target:** 5 posts/week, fixed schedule, 90-day minimum before evaluation.

## Layer 4: Copy & Engagement (Jasmin Alić)

**Post anatomy:**
```
Line 1: Hook (stop the scroll)
Lines 2–4: Agitation (make the problem felt)
Lines 5–10: Value (actionable insight)
Line 11: Pivot (reframe or revelation)
Final line: CTA (comment prompt, not link)
```

**Comment strategy:**
- Engage 10 ICP accounts before posting daily
- Reply to every comment within 30 minutes of posting
- Use Lara's "3+1 comment" technique: add 3 follow-up comments to your own post

## Layer 5: Distribution (Ross Simmonds + Amanda Natividad)

**Create once, distribute forever:**
1. Write one deep asset (newsletter, podcast, long post)
2. Repurpose into: LinkedIn posts, email, short video, carousel
3. Track dark social signals (replies, DMs, "I saw your post" mentions) — not just clicks

**Zero-click rule:**
- Full value lives inside the LinkedIn post
- External links in first comment only
- Posts must be complete enough to share without context

## Layer 6: Message Clarity (Peep Laja)

**3-second clarity test (score 1–5):**
- Can a stranger understand the hook in 3 seconds?
- Is there one clear takeaway?
- Is jargon stripped without losing precision?

**Fix order:** Positioning → clarity → volume. Never reverse.

## Layer 7: Algorithm Mechanics (Richard van der Blom + Lara Acosta)

**2025 algorithm priorities:**
- Relationship depth > follower count
- Profile as AI prompt — specific headline = matched distribution
- Relevancy over recency — posts resurface weeks later
- Small accounts have structural advantage in new system

**5-step action plan (Acosta + van der Blom):**
1. Fix profile — one specific ICP statement in headline
2. Pick your lane — one topic + adjacent subtopics
3. Match format to your natural communication style
4. Build engagement habits with target accounts
5. Post consistently — perfection is the enemy

## Layer 8: Founder-Led Brand (Dave Gerhardt)

**Exit Five model applied to B2B SaaS:**
- Founder posts on LinkedIn → builds familiarity
- Weekly newsletter → builds trust (cadence + replies > open rates)
- Community/membership → monetizes audience
- Email extends the trust loop (see `/research/youtube-transcripts/dave-gerhardt/`)

**Dave's content principles:**
- Nobody wants to read your marketing — write with inbox empathy
- Give value before asking for anything
- Founder voice > corporate voice, always

## Layer 9: Email + LinkedIn Integration

From Dave Gerhardt's Exit Five Live session:

| Channel | Job | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Awareness + familiarity | Comments, profile views, DMs |
| Newsletter | Trust + nurture | Reply rate, qualitative feedback |
| Email sequences | Conversion | Pipeline influenced, buyer pre-purchase engagement |

**Tactical bridges:**
- Repurpose top LinkedIn posts into newsletter "personal note" sections
- Use product data for personalized email campaigns (Quo year-in-review model)
- Newsjack industry events on LinkedIn same-day + email to list (Tyler's system)

## Measurement: Signal vs. Vanity

**Vanity metrics:** Impressions, follower count, likes
**Signal metrics:** Profile views from ICP, DMs, reply rate, "I saw your post" mentions, demo requests mentioning content, email reply rate
