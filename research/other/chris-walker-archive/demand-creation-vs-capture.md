**Status:** Historical reference — Walker exited B2B marketing July 2025
**Referenced by:** Katelyn Bourgoin (Post 03), modern B2B demand gen practitioners

---

## Core Framework

Most B2B marketing budgets are allocated to **demand capture** — intercepting buyers who are already actively searching for a solution. This includes:
- Google Ads on high-intent keywords ("best ATS software")
- SEO for comparison pages
- Retargeting campaigns
- G2/Capterra optimization

**Demand creation** builds awareness and preference *before* the buyer enters active search mode:
- LinkedIn organic content
- Podcasts and thought leadership
- Community building
- Named problem frameworks
- Founder-led brand

---

## The Budget Imbalance

Walker argued most B2B SaaS companies spend 90%+ on capture and <10% on creation — then wonder why CAC keeps rising and pipelines dry up.

**Why capture gets more budget:** It's measurable. Google Analytics shows clicks. LinkedIn impressions don't show up in attribution reports.

**Why creation matters more long-term:** Capture competes on price and features. Creation competes on trust and preference — and has lower marginal cost once content compounds.

## The Buying Journey Connection (Katelyn Bourgoin JTBD)

Walker's demand creation maps directly to Bourgoin's buying timeline:

```
DEMAND CREATION covers:
  Stage 1: Trigger event awareness
  Stage 2: Passive looking

DEMAND CAPTURE covers:
  Stage 3: Active looking
  Stage 4: Decision
```

LinkedIn organic content targets stages 1–2. By the time a buyer reaches stage 3, the brand that created demand during passive looking has a massive advantage.

## LinkedIn's Role in Demand Creation

| Tactic | Demand type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Named problem posts | Creation | "Spreadsheet Hiring Syndrome" |
| Trigger event stories | Creation | "You open your spreadsheet Monday morning..." |
| Thought leadership | Creation | Industry trend analysis |
| Customer proof posts | Creation + Capture | Before/after case studies |
| Product comparison posts | Capture | "Why teams switch from [competitor]" |
| Demo/trial CTAs | Capture | Direct conversion posts |

**Rule:** 80% creation, 20% capture on LinkedIn. Capture happens better in email sequences and on your website.

---

## Measurement Gap Connection

Demand creation happens primarily in **dark social** channels. Standard attribution undercounts creation and overcounts capture — creating a systematic bias toward cutting the budget that actually builds long-term pipeline.

**Fix:** Measure creation through dark social signals (DMs, "saw your post" mentions, branded search volume) not through last-touch attribution alone.

---

## B2B SaaS Application

**Weekly content mix:**
- Mon–Thu: Demand creation (problem naming, trigger events, identity posts, frameworks)
- Fri: Light capture (customer story, soft CTA)
- Never: Hard sell posts more than 1x/week

**Budget reframe for leadership:**
"LinkedIn isn't a lead gen channel. It's a demand creation channel. Leads are the lagging indicator, not the leading one."
