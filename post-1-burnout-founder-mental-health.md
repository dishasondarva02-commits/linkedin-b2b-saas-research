# Post 1 — Burnout / Founder Mental Health (Top Performer)
**Author:** Tyler Denk (Co-founder & CEO, beehiiv)
**Source:** Reconstructed from coaching session with Lara Acosta (YouTube)
**Content Type:** Personality / story post — total addressable market

## Why It's a Top Performer (per data analysis)
This was Tyler's best-performing post across 200+ analyzed. Key reasons:
- **Total addressable market appeal**: Anyone who works can relate to burnout — not just email marketers or newsletter founders
- **Selfie / personal photo** paired with the copy gives immediate human context
- **Short and punchy** — follows the "keep it as short as the story allows" principle
- Topped the "personality" tier in Lara's audit framework

## Reconstructed Post Structure

**Hook:** [Short, honest line about hitting a wall]

**Body (short — 3–5 lines):**
- What was happening at the time (context)
- What it felt like (emotion)
- What changed (transformation beat)

**Closing:** One-line takeaway or question that any founder/professional can answer

**Image:** Selfie — candid, not polished

## Psychological Triggers Used
- **Shared vulnerability**: CEOs of $30M+ companies burning out = permission for readers to acknowledge their own burnout
- **Underdog framing**: Even successful founders struggle → positions Tyler as relatable, not just aspirational
- **"Total addressable market" content**: Subject (burnout) has zero prerequisite knowledge — any professional on LinkedIn is the audience

## B2B SaaS Applications
- One "founder mental health" or "what I learned the hard way" post per week drives disproportionate reach
- Use selfies (not stock photos, not graphics) for these posts — authenticity signal
- These posts expand top-of-funnel: new readers find you through the human story, then discover the product
